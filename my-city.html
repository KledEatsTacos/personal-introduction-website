City

Jeddah, known as the 'Bride of the Red Sea', is a vibrant city on Saudi Arabia's coast. It offers a unique blend of historic districts and modern architecture. As the gateway for pilgrimages to Mecca and Medina, it's a city of cultural significance and diversity.

About City

Jeddah is a port city located along the Red Sea coast. As of 2022, it has a population of about 3,751,722 people, making it the second largest city in Saudi Arabia (After the capital Riyadh). Jeddah is also a gateway to the two most holy cities in Islam, Mecca and Medina. The city is known for its historic districts, modern architecture, and vibrant culture. It is a popular destination for tourists and pilgrims alike.

Historical Jeddah

Historical Jeddah is situated on the eastern shore of the Red Sea. From the 7th century AD, it was established as a major port for Indian Ocean trade routes, channeling goods to Mecca. It was also the gateway for Muslim pilgrims to Mecca who arrived by sea. These twin roles saw the city develop into a thriving multicultural center, characterized by a distinctive architectural tradition, including tower houses built in the late 19th century by the city's mercantile elites, and combining Red Sea coastal coral building traditions with influences and crafts from along the trade routes. Within a defensive wall that was built during Ottoman rule, the old city of Jeddah, Al-Balad, was divided into districts, or Haras, where business and trade centered around traditional souks, or market places, and khans, covered markets that were generally connected to shops.

Main Sights

Jeddah is renowned for its unique sculptures and structures that adorn its streets and public spaces, showcasing a blend of contemporary artistry and traditional influences, adding to the city's distinct charm and cultural significance.

Open Air Art

Jeddah is home to a number of open-air art installations that are scattered throughout the city. These sculptures and structures are created by local and international artists, and they add to the city's vibrant and diverse cultural landscape. They are mostly in the shape of famous roundabouts all over the city.

King Fahd Fountain

The King Fahd Fountain is a major landmark in Jeddah. It is the tallest fountain in the world, reaching a height of 312 meters. The fountain is located in the Red Sea and can be seen from various parts of the city. It was built in 1985 and is named after King Fahd bin Abdulaziz Al Saud, the former King of Saudi Arabia.

Flagpole

The Jeddah Flagpole is the tallest flagpole in the world, standing at a height of 170 meters. It is located on the King Abdullah Square in Jeddah and was inaugurated in 2014. The flagpole flies the flag of Saudi Arabia, which measures 49.5 meters in length and 33 meters in width.
